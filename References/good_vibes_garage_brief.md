**For handoff to Claude Code**

---

## The Person

**Dr. Elliott Hedman** — builder, teacher, researcher. The "Dr." matters and must be present from the first impression. It's the credential that unlocks credibility with the employer/corporate audience.

---

## The Brand

**Good Vibes Garage** — a personal platform, upcoming podcast, and body of work about AI, vibe coding, and expanding who gets to create things.

The name should do real work: it's warm (Vibes), grounded (Garage), and signals that real building happens here — not just talk.

## The Two Audiences (both matter, weighted differently)

### Audience 1: Vibe Coding Curious (Eyeballs — primary)
- Developers, creatives, learners who are excited about or curious about AI-assisted building
- They want to feel like they found their person — someone living at the edge of this shift
- Hook: the podcast, the SXSW talk, the experiments and featured work

### Audience 2: Employers / Corporate Buyers (Money — secondary)
- Companies who want to upskill employees in vibe coding / AI-assisted creation
- They need to see authority, credibility, and a track record fast
- Hook: "Dr.", SXSW talk, teaching practice, "Bring Elliott in" CTA

**Design implication:** The hero and first scroll must speak to Audience 1 emotionally. The credibility signals (Dr., SXSW, featured work) serve Audience 2 without alienating Audience 1.

---

## Site Phase

This site is being built **pre-podcast launch** (~1 month out). The podcast (also called Good Vibes Garage) will be on YouTube.

**Phase 1 goal (now):** Establish credibility, showcase the SXSW talk/article, capture email subscribers before the podcast drops.

**Phase 2 goal (post-launch):** The latest podcast episode becomes the front door. The site was designed for this from the start — the email capture section converts to a "latest episode" hero with minimal redesign.

**Most important conversion right now: email sign-up.** "Get notified when the podcast drops" or equivalent. This needs to be prominent without being desperate.

## Visual Direction

### Mood
Creative & edgy — but grounded and approachable. Not cold. Not corporate. Not startup-generic.

Think: a brilliant professor who also runs a maker space. Authority + warmth.

### Background
**Light / white** — approachable, readable, editorial feel. Not the typical dark-mode dev aesthetic.

### Accent Color
**Warm & energetic** — oranges, reds, ambers, golds. One dominant accent used with intention, not splattered everywhere. Use it on CTAs, hover states, and key typographic moments.

### Typography
Avoid Inter, Roboto, Arial, Space Grotesk — anything generic.

Go for:
- A **distinctive display font** for headlines — something with character (editorial, slightly unconventional, confident)
- A **clean readable body font** — approachable, not sterile

Pair should feel like: *smart magazine meets maker culture.*

### Motion & Feel
- Staggered entrance animations on page load (subtle, purposeful — not flashy)
- Hover states that feel alive
- Scroll-triggered reveals for sections below the fold
- Nothing that feels like a template

### Layout
Not a standard top-to-bottom stack of boxes. Use:
- Asymmetry where appropriate
- Generous negative space in the hero
- A layout that feels *designed*, not assembled

## Copy Principles

1. **Always "Dr. Elliott Hedman"** on first reference. "Elliott" is fine after that.
2. **No filler openings.** Don't start sections with "AI is changing everything." Start with a specific idea or a question.
3. **Write like a smart person talking**, not like a landing page generator.
4. **The podcast isn't launched yet** — never write as if it exists. Write as if it's coming and it's going to matter.
5. **"Vibe coding"** should be used naturally — not over-explained, not avoided. It belongs here.
6. **The through-line is democratization of creation.** Elliott has always been working on who gets to build things. Vibe coding is the latest and most powerful chapter of that story — not a pivot.
7. **Old credentials serve the new story.** MIT, IDEO, Google are mentioned once, briefly, as context — not as the headline. They answer "why should I trust this guy?" without making the site feel like a CV.

## Technical Notes for Claude Code

- **Single-page site**, full scroll, with smooth anchor navigation
- **Email capture** needs a real or placeholder form — Mailchimp / ConvertKit / Beehiiv integration to be added later; build the UI with a placeholder action
- **YouTube embed** — placeholder `<iframe>` for the SXSW talk, easy to swap URL
- **Mobile-first responsive** — both audiences will hit this on their phones
- **Performance matters** — light, fast, no unnecessary JS bloat
- **Fonts:** Load from Google Fonts or similar — avoid system fonts
- Animate with CSS transitions/keyframes where possible; keep JS minimal
