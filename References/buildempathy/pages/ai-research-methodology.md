## Primary Work: "Show My Work"

Developed to address ChatGPT usage in classrooms. Through iterative prototyping and 100+ teacher interviews, three key findings emerged:

1. Accountability should be student-driven, not just externally imposed
2. Writing instruction requires focus on process rather than final output
3. Real-time AI feedback upon paper completion serves teachers effectively

---

## Three-Step Research Methodology

**Step 1 — Digital Prototyping**  
mPath builds functional test software in 1-2 weeks using Google Apps Script. Their prototype detected copy-pasting and prompted student reflection, evolving toward student-facing design and paragraph-level analysis.

**Step 2 — Classroom Co-creation**  
Teachers video record themselves using tools in live classrooms, followed by structured interviews. The company employs emotion sensors and eye-tracking technology for deeper usage insights.

**Step 3 — Rapid Iteration**  
Products undergo iterative refinement after each teacher interaction. Initial "Show Your Work" shifted from teacher-facing to student-centered accountability, later integrating ChatGPT for personalized reflection questions.

---

## Company Approach (15-Year Foundation)

**Representation:** Partner with Boys & Girls Club to include underrepresented students, particularly those academically behind.

**Measurement:** Utilize eye-tracking and emotional sensors developed by Dr. Hedman at MIT.

**Lightning-Fast Iteration:** Weekly build-test-refine cycles translate teacher needs into testable features.
