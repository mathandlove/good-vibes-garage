# Empowering Parents to Teach Reading (Toddlers Can Read App)

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/empoweringparents  
**Client:** Toddlers Can Read (founded by Spencer)  
**Sector:** App Design, Education, Parents, Motivation  
**Role:** Design and Development Lead

---

## Design Review Findings

Initial analysis revealed three key issues:
1. Video-heavy onboarding was intimidating for parents
2. Activities lacked clear, actionable guidance
3. Achievement badges focused on video completion rather than meaningful literacy goals

---

## Design Principles Established

- **People Learn by Doing:** Active participation beats passive consumption
- **Make Parent Actions Tangible:** Clear, specific activities trump abstract suggestions
- **Badges Need Meaningful Goals:** Rewards should align with user objectives, not platform metrics

---

## Prototype Development

Team brainstormed collaboratively in Dallas, creating Prototype #1 — built as an interactive web-based digital prototype using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for easy sharing and adaptive functionality.

---

## Research Methodology

Rather than instructing parents to use the app, researchers distributed it as a bonus, allowing genuine usage patterns to emerge during open-ended interviews.

---

## Key Research Findings: Two Parent Profiles

**Planner Parents:** Highly organized, information-seeking, typically stay-at-home. Embrace comprehensive resources and detailed planning.

**Dreamer Parents:** The larger demographic. Desire to help their children but abandon efforts when obstacles appear. Need clear pathways forward. **This was Spencer's primary growth opportunity.**

---

## Design Principles for Dreamer Parents

### Drips, Not Firehoses
Bite-sized, sequential learning beats overwhelming information dumps. Daily activities introduce one concept, allowing parents to build confidence through practice.

### Soft Starts
Before introducing educational content, establish routines and gameplay structures. Begin with foundational activities like animal sounds or shapes to normalize the experience.

### Eliminate Parent Choice
Parents reported that multiple options created decision paralysis. They preferred: "Just tell me that I am playing The Floor Is Lava while working on the 'B' sound." Clear direction replaced empowering choice.

### Hackable Tasks
Activities should be predetermined, but parents need flexibility to modify them when situations require adjustment. Optional variations prevent rigidity while maintaining simplicity.

### Leave AI in the Background
FAQ sections went unused — parents found self-directed help-seeking cognitively demanding. However, background algorithms making activity recommendations were enthusiastically received.

### Mandatory Fun
Child enthusiasm determined parent app adoption more than any other factor. Parents needed support in making learning enjoyable, not fighting resistance.

---

## Outcomes & Conclusions

Effective parent-directed learning apps must remove the burden of expertise, planning, and entertainment. Parents possess incredible potential for literacy instruction when technology removes friction and builds confidence.
