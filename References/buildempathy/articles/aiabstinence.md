# Why AI Abstinence Policies Are Backfiring

**Author:** Elliott Hedman  
**Date:** April 8  
**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/human-ai-interactions/aiabstinence  
**Publication:** mPath

---

## Full Article Content

Hedman argues that school district policies prohibiting student AI use — termed "AI Abstinence" — are ineffective and counterproductive. His research of 200 high school and college students revealed that nearly all use AI for assignments, often paraphrasing generated content to evade detection tools.

### Key Findings

**Widespread AI Adoption**  
Every interviewed student employed AI for classwork, with most using it for every assignment. One student compared the habit to substance use: "I try it once, then the next day you want more. Soon, it's just how you do things."

**Why Students Embrace AI**

1. **Detection Failures:** AI detection tools like Turnitin claim 99% accuracy only for unedited text. Paraphrased AI writing appears human-authored, bypassing safeguards.

2. **Academic Benefits:** Students report improved grades and efficiency. One noted that "ChatGPT helps me sound more intelligent — like I really know what I'm talking about."

3. **Perceived Learning:** Students believe AI enhances their education, not diminishes it, viewing it as standard practice.

4. **Lack of Dialogue:** Teachers avoid transparent conversations about appropriate AI use due to relationship concerns, leaving students to develop their own ethical frameworks.

### The Critical Thinking Problem

Hedman emphasizes that writing struggles catalyze genuine learning. Assignments requiring self-reflection, critical analysis, and voice development lose pedagogical value when AI generates content. He demonstrated this through a Socratic questioning tool: students who submitted AI-written papers consistently failed to explain their reasoning or demonstrate content mastery.

### Proposed Solutions

Rather than prohibition, Hedman suggests innovative approaches:
- Socratic dialogue assignments documenting reasoning
- AI-led podcasts requiring authentic student voice
- Level Up tool promoting self-reflection

He calls for moving "beyond AI Abstinence — and into a more honest, innovative, and empowering future for student learning."
