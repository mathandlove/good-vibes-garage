# 3 Pillars of Teacher Adoption for Edtech

**Author:** Elliott Hedman  
**Date:** March 11  
**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/human-ai-interactions/teacher-adoption  
**Publication:** mPath

---

## Full Article Content

This article examines why teachers often fail to adopt educational technology despite initial interest. Through three months of research with Classcraft, a behavioral management software, the author identified three critical pillars for successful teacher adoption.

### The Three Pillars

**Pillar 1: Easy to Understand**  
The original Classcraft onboarding overwhelmed teachers with excessive features and content. The revised approach removes non-essential elements, emphasizes learning through hands-on practice, and limits instructional videos to 2-3 minutes maximum.

**Pillar 2: Easy to Use**  
The interface was simplified from requiring knowledge of complex elements (teams, levels, HP, XP, etc.) to a straightforward plus-button system for awarding points. Key strategies include clearly defining core functionality, providing guided guardrails, demonstrating classroom implementation, and encouraging early customization.

**Pillar 3: Easy to Sell to Students**  
Teachers evaluate whether students will adopt software before committing themselves. The solution involved creating engaging student preview content, highlighting in-class rewards during teacher onboarding, and eliminating account creation requirements on day one.

### Key Insight

According to the article, "Making easy-to-use software isn't just about increasing user numbers but also about making education solutions accessible to more students."

The author emphasizes that effective edtech design must accommodate all teachers, not just tech-savvy early adopters.
