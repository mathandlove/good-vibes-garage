# Reading and Play with the LEGO Group

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/designinglegoplay  
**Scraped:** April 2026

---

## Representation
Working with Denver Public Schools, the organization tested and selected a cohort of resistant readers to design experiences merging play and reading. Families visiting LEGO stores across Colorado were subsequently recruited.

## Methodology
Children received a book and toy, then engaged in reading and play without adult supervision or instructions. Research discovered "most children do not storytell with their toys without the prompting of an adult." Emotion sensors revealed that interactions with family members facilitate story-making engagement in children.

## Iteration
Over 30 different versions of Reading Play books were developed. Initially, "Students wouldn't even open our first books." Various prototypes documented at LEGO Prototype Stories.

## Outcome
The LEGO Group launched a new book line called LEGO Play Stories to enhance play experiences. Published article "Learning Through Play" documents the research findings.
