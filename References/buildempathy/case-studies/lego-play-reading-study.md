# LEGO + Play — Reading and Play Study

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/designinglegoplay  
**Client:** The LEGO Group  
**Partners:** Denver Public Schools

---

## Representation

mPath partnered with Denver Public Schools to engage struggling readers. The team "tested and selected a cohort of resistant readers to help us design experiences that incorporate play and reading simultaneously." They also recruited families visiting LEGO stores across Colorado.

---

## Methodology

Researchers observed children with books and toys in unsupervised settings.

**Key discovery:** Most children lack spontaneous storytelling play without adult guidance.

Testing with emotion sensors revealed that "interactions with a family member help children engage in story-making."

---

## Iteration

The design process involved substantial refinement — over 30 prototype versions of reading-play books were developed before achieving engagement. Students "wouldn't even open our first books."

---

## Outcome

- The LEGO Group launched a new product line called **LEGO Play Stories**
- mPath published research findings in "Learning Through Play"
- Research details how reading integrates with playful engagement
