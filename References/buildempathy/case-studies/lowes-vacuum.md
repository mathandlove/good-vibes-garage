# Lowe's — Improving the Vacuum-Buying Experience

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/lowes  
**Client:** Lowe's  
**Sector:** Retail, UX Design

---

## Key Outcome

"Lowe's implemented over 150 of mPath's design suggestions" and achieved a **9% sales increase** through an in-store randomized control test, plus improved customer satisfaction and likelihood to recommend.

---

## Team Members
Adrianne Cooley, Misa Misono, Brian Demers, Judy Riley, Parry Koriath, Elliott Hedman

---

## Three Core Design Interventions

### 1. Simplify Messaging
- **Before:** Overwhelming, disorganized signage that prevented engagement
- **After:** Clear labeling ("Best vacuum for home use") and interactive prompts ("Try me out") addressing shoppers' primary question: which vacuum suits home use

### 2. Add Interaction
- **Before:** Clean design alone yielded only 5/10 ratings despite addressing professional aesthetics and accessibility
- **After:** Installed interactive tennis-ball suction demonstration, dramatically improving satisfaction ratings to "the best they ever had at Lowe's"

### 3. Simplify Choices
- **Before:** 14 vacuum options overwhelmed customers unable to differentiate
- **After:** Reorganized into three categories (small, medium, large storage) aligned with customer mental models

---

## Core Insight

"The overarching theme in our design work was that shoppers were not feeling confident" in purchase decisions.

---

## Research Methods
- Fake store prototype testing with rapid iteration
- Suction physics experimentation
- MOXO sensor engagement tracking
