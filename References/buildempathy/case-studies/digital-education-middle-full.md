# Mapping the Emotional Experience of Digital Education (Middle School)

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/digitalmiddle  
**Scraped:** April 2026

**Team:** Garrett Hedman, Meredith Wilson, Christine Zanchi, Elysa Greenberger, Sara Stump

---

## Overview
mPath conducted observational research at three Title 1 schools to understand how middle school students experience digital learning software and what motivates their engagement.

---

## Key Findings

**Grades and Motivation:**
"Grades mark completion" initially, causing students to skip learning moments. A 6th grade student fails to be motivated during a lesson, but cares much more during the quiz they are being evaluated on.

**Teacher Involvement:**
Classrooms with strong teacher engagement showed the most impact. "Some students will stop using educational software as soon as their teacher stops monitoring them."

**Audio-Based Instruction:**
Middle school students resisted passive audio lessons and preferred interactive problem-solving. Students were avoiding the moments where they had to think or struggle through a problem, instead going to moments of high arousal: specifically getting an answer correct.

**Psychological Barriers:**
"In Middle School students start doubting themselves and thinking themselves stupid when lessons get hard."

**Motivation Challenges:**
"Grades had squashed most children's natural desire to learn."

---

## Recommendations

1. Integrate grades throughout lessons, not just at completion
2. Increase teacher visibility and involvement in software use
3. Replace passive audio instruction with interactive, pull-based learning
4. Incorporate simple games as motivational elements
