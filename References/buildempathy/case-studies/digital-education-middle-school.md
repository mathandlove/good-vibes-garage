# Mapping the Emotional Experience of Digital Education (Middle School)

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/digitalmiddle  
**Research:** Three Title 1 schools; also observed children playing video games at home

---

## Team
Garrett Hedman, Meredith Wilson, Christine Zanchi, Elysa Greenberger, Sara Stump

---

## Key Findings

**Grades and Learning Motivation**  
Students prioritized passing lessons and receiving grades without connecting performance to actual learning. Students "skipped the learning and feedback moments, not paying attention to moments where they could learn."  
Recommendation: Integrate grades throughout lessons rather than only at completion.

**Teacher Involvement Impact**  
Classrooms with active teacher engagement showed the most success. When "teachers were heavily involved with how their students used their software," outcomes improved significantly. In classes with substitute teachers, software adoption was minimal.

**Audio Instruction Ineffectiveness**  
Most lessons used passive audio instruction, but middle school students preferred direct problem-solving. Recommendation: Shift to "interactive problems as the start of learning" and allow students to "pull" written information when stuck.

---

## Notable Observations
- Students avoided challenging moments, seeking high-arousal experiences from correct answers
- Grades had diminished students' intrinsic motivation to learn
- Simple games proved surprisingly motivating despite initial concerns
- Student engagement increased substantially during graded assessments
