# Teaching the Concepts of Addition and Subtraction Digitally

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/addition  
**Scraped:** April 2026

---

## Core Challenge
Schools had taught equations like 2+3=5 but not their meaning. Few children understood the word "subtraction."

**Key Outcome:** Interactive "sticky stories" proved most effective for teaching new concepts and maintaining engagement.

---

## Three Major Principles

### 1. Emphasis on Vocabulary
- **Before:** Minimal vocabulary use caused children to miss unfamiliar terms
- **After:** Repeated exposure (the word "subtract" appeared hundreds of times), with children saying it aloud and interactive reinforcement ("_Beep, beep_. Subtraction in progress")

### 2. Sticky Story
- **Before:** Traditional marble-in-a-bag model lacked engagement
- **After:** Story-based narratives with child interaction (example: hats falling off) created stronger engagement

### 3. Consistency
- **Before:** Single context (balloons only) led to misconceptions
- **After:** Multiple contexts and simulations prevented unintended pattern identification
