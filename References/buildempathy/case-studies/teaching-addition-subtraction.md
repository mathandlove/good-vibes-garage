# Teaching Addition and Subtraction Digitally

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/addition

---

## Key Outcome

Interactive "sticky stories" proved most effective for teaching concepts while maintaining student engagement.

---

## Three Core Findings

**1. Vocabulary Emphasis**
- Before: Minimal use of new terminology caused children to miss key concepts and remain unaware of terms like "subtraction"
- After: Repeated vocabulary integration ("subtract" repeated hundreds of times) with active student participation and auditory reinforcement improved learning

**2. Narrative-Based Learning**
- Before: Traditional demonstrations using marbles in bags lacked engagement, even among staff
- After: Story-driven simulations where children actively influenced narratives (such as hats falling with addition) significantly increased engagement

**3. Contextual Diversity**
- Before: Single-context examples (all balloon-based) led to incorrect pattern identification
- After: Multiple contexts and simulations prevented unintended associations and reinforced accurate concept understanding

---

## Client Feedback

Stakeholders emphasized learning from each engagement phase and appreciated the collaborative, iterative approach. One noted: "Repetition isn't a turn-off but a useful teaching tool."
