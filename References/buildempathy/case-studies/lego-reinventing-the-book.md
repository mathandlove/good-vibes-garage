# Reinventing the Book with the LEGO Group

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/legoreading  
**Client:** Publishing within The LEGO Group  
**Sector:** Toys, Play, Education, Publishing  
**Role:** Design Director and Research Lead  
**Scope:** Managed 10 designers over 1 year

---

## Problem Statement

The LEGO Publishing Group discovered that children purchased books primarily for the included bricks, with minimal actual reading engagement. Early testing confirmed books remained unread.

---

## Research Methodology: Denver Public Schools Prototyping

**Participant Group:** Second to fourth-grade struggling readers who disliked reading

**Testing Approach:**
- 11+ iterative prototype cycles
- Co-creation with reluctant readers
- Eye tracking and skin conductance sensors for observation
- Student feedback directed each iteration

---

## Prototype Evolution & Key Findings

**Prototype #1: Books that Direct Play** ("Escape the Volcano")
- Instruction: Place dinosaurs to tell stories
- Result: Students ignored books, chose play only
- Finding: "If students don't have a reason to read, they will choose to only play."

**Prototype #5: Books Contain Instructions** (Play-Doh Castle)
- Strategy: Removed assembly instructions; book required for building
- Result: Most students created alternative projects; one student read to become an architect
- Finding: "Students will shift their play behavior in an effort to avoid reading."

**Prototype #7: Play That Requires Reading** (72-page slime-making story)
- Strategy: Made reading essential to desired outcome
- Result: 100% reading completion
- Finding: "If reading is essential to play, the book will be valued and read."

---

## Critical Pivot Point

After testing multiple book-building combinations, the researcher left test books behind during a session. Student "Asher" and siblings abandoned LEGO sets after construction completion, transitioning to video games instead.

**Key Realization:** "Children don't want books to help them with BUILDING. They want help with STORYTELLING."

---

## Solution Development

Two-week intensive design sprint with LEGO Publishing team revealed children's actual need: character information (names, hobbies, fears, goals) to enable extended storytelling play.

---

## Five Design Principles for Success

1. **Provide a Meaningful Background to Inspire Play** — Include character profiles and backstories for play context

2. **Ensure Connections to Toys** — Stories must be replicable with children's existing toys

3. **Use Open-Ended Prompts for Storytelling** — Questions like "What kind of environment would the character live in?" stimulate imagination

4. **Promote Digital Documentation** — Incorporate photography prompts for self-reflection and story retelling

5. **Encourage Social Interactions** — Include adults, siblings, or peers to enrich language development

---

## Outcomes

- **Product Launch:** LEGO Group successfully published storytelling books with positive market reception
- **Publication:** LEGO Foundation and ISTE published research article: *Learning Through Play*
- **Commercial Product:** Play Stories available for purchase on LEGO.com

**Vision:** Research team envisions a future where every toy includes supplementary books enhancing character storytelling and extended play engagement.
