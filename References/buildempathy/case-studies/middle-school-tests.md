# Motivating Middle Schoolers to Take Tests

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/middleschooltests  

---

## Team
Meredith Wilson, Christine Zanchi, Elliott Hedman

---

## Problem Statement

Students were not engaging seriously with diagnostic tests, answering randomly. This resulted in inaccurate school scores and inappropriate content placement.

---

## Key Findings

**1. Narrator Credibility Matters Most**
- High school students commanded middle schoolers' attention better than animated characters
- Students distrusted messages from narrators who didn't genuinely believe in them
- Real student stories were highly memorable compared to entertainment-focused content

**2. Messaging Should Emphasize Growth**
- "Try hard" messaging was ineffective
- Students responded better to "I don't give up" and "proving yourself" frameworks
- Downplaying challenge ("this will be easy") backfired when students encountered difficult problems

**3. Anxiety, Not Apathy, Drives Disengagement**
- Eye tracking and MOXO sensors revealed students quit after encountering difficult questions
- Fast guessing was a coping mechanism for anxiety, not lack of motivation
- Normalizing mistakes ("getting something wrong is okay") was more effective than emphasizing correctness

---

## Solution

Produced messaging featuring high school student narrators paired with teacher support, centered on empowerment and resilience rather than difficulty minimization.
