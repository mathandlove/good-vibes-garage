# Creating a Great LEGO Building Experience (LEGO Technic Digital Instructions)

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/legotechnic  
**Client:** The LEGO Group  
**Research Method:** Observational research with 10 boys wearing MOXO Sensors

---

## Primary Outcome

The research revealed that effective building experiences extend beyond instruction comprehension. Successful design must support children's ability to "reflect, regulate, and feel a sense of achievement."

---

## Three Major Design Insights

### 1. Visual Complexity Creates Stress
**Finding:** Animated 3D rotations and piece-flying animations — intended as intuitive aids — actually increased user stress. Children couldn't follow rapid animations and would arbitrarily rotate models when confused, compounding building difficulties.

**Solution:** Remove animations; provide static, clear instruction views.

### 2. Embrace Error Correction
**Finding:** Despite meticulous instruction design, mistakes were inevitable. Children became "lost and defeated" upon discovering errors.

**Solution:** Insert reflection checkpoints asking children to verify gear functionality, enabling early error detection and reducing frustration.

### 3. Parents as Essential Support
**Finding:** The original design excluded parental involvement, yet research showed children demonstrated "less stress and more capability" with parental assistance.

**Solution:** Redesign instructions to explicitly invite parent participation and facilitate collaborative building.
