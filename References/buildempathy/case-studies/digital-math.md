# Understanding the Digital Math Experience

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/digitalmath  
**Research:** Observational study, 19 children learning double and single-digit addition

---

## Core Finding

Classroom teaching methods fail in isolated digital learning environments.

---

## Key Design Changes

**Animations**
- Before: Long animated sequences with movement, jokes, and instruction as primary teaching tools
- After: Short introductory animations only; longer clips caused disengagement and missed instructions after ~5 seconds

**Feedback Systems**
- Before: Minimal feedback with generic "try again" responses; answers provided after three failed attempts
- After: Dynamic feedback became the primary teaching mechanism, helping children learn from mistakes and reducing helplessness

**Teaching Interfaces**
- Before: Interfaces went untaught; children struggled independently, interpreting interface difficulties as math deficiency
- After: Modeling interface behavior explicitly (demonstrating button functions through character interactions) integrated math and tool instruction

---

## Key Insights

"Answering questions was highly engaging" — established the foundation for mPath's "The Feeling of Learning" framework. Children equipped with proper scaffolding performed significantly better than those facing inadequately designed interfaces.

---

## Client Feedback

Partners praised the rapid prototyping approach and actionable insights, particularly noting how observing student disengagement with animations provided powerful validation for design changes.
