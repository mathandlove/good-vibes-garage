# Mapping the Emotional Experience of Digital Education (Elementary)

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/digitalelementary  
**Research:** Three Title 1 schools; also observed children playing video games at home

---

## Team
Garrett Hedman, Meredith Wilson, Christine Zanchi, Elysa Greenberger, Sara Stump

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Integrate Games with Learning Content
**Problem:** Educational software treated games as rewards after lesson completion, which children avoided.  
**Solution:** Combine gaming mechanics directly with learning. When students encountered math and reading within games, they showed greater engagement and asked for more academic content. Key observation: "learning was fun, and the games were taking that away."

### 2. Embrace Appropriate Challenge Levels
**Problem:** Students complained lessons felt "too easy," yet data showed they were answering incorrectly. Low difficulty settings provided false confidence.  
**Solution:** Create problems with meaningful weight or stakes. "Things will always feel easy when there are no stakes."

### 3. Reframe Failure as Learning Opportunity
**Problem:** Traditional approaches provided immediate correct answers after mistakes.  
**Solution:** Use incorrect responses as teachable moments. "Kids are most ready to learn when they answer wrong, but teaching rarely starts there."

---

## Research Insights
Children's perspectives on mastery and learning differ significantly from traditional educational assumptions. Emotional engagement drives educational software effectiveness.
