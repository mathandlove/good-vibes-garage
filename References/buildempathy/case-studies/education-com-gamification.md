# Gamification + Learning with Education.com

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/designingeducationcom  
**Client:** Education.com  
**Sector:** Gamification, Educational Software, Homeschool

---

## Representation

The project focused on homeschooled students across the United States, observing how they naturally interacted with the software in home environments.

---

## Methodology

Researchers discovered that without adult supervision, younger users became disoriented within the platform and struggled to navigate toward appropriate lessons independently.

---

## Iteration

The team developed **15 paper prototype games using PowerPoint**, which students tested remotely. Through iterative refinement based on user feedback, the prototypes evolved significantly. By the final stage, "students were saying these games were their favorite games."

---

## Outcome

Education.com established a systematic framework and design methodology for creating engaging games that encourage repeated user engagement.

**Published finding:** The Gamification Rubric — open-source resource originally at wonderstories.app/research/engaginggamesrubric

---

## Key Finding

Structured gamification principles, informed by actual student behavior and preferences, can transform educational software into experiences learners actively want to revisit.
