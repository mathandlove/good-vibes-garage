# Teaching Phonemic Awareness Digitally

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/addition-lbmkg (phonemic awareness page)  
**Scraped:** April 2026

---

## Core Challenge
Phonemic awareness is best taught in small groups with teachers modeling and giving direct feedback to children. The client needed to reinvent this process digitally.

**Outcome:** Ready-to-implement lessons were delivered to teach phonemic awareness. Early research demonstrated that with clear feedback, children could focus and dedicate time to practicing sounds.

---

## Key Improvements

### 1. Challenges Linked to Agency and Helping
- **Before:** Quiz-like challenges ("Put the word that starts with B back in the box") — children struggled to find correct answers
- **After:** Challenges reframed around agency ("Help feed the dog") — motivation to help substantially reduced anxiety around difficult challenges

### 2. Say It Out Loud at Every Moment
- **Before:** Children only spoke aloud once at lesson beginning; inconsistent with classroom phonemic awareness activities
- **After:** Kindergarten students say everything aloud — every phoneme, word, and blend. Reflects how young students don't differentiate between speaking and computer interactions

### 3. Tea Ball Starts for Interfaces
- **Before:** "Feed the Monster" activity immediately showed a dish — children didn't understand what to do
- **After:** First simulation rounds use concepts children already understand ("Feed the Monster" started with differentiating animal sounds)
