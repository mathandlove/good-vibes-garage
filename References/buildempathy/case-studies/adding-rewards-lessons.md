# Adding Rewards to Digital Lessons

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/learningrewards  
**Client:** Boys & Girls Club

---

## Team
Garrett Hedman, Meredith Wilson, Christine Zanchi, Elysa Greenberger, Elliott Hedman

---

## Key Outcome

Research shifted the client's strategy from building a complex avatar system to implementing immediate, localized rewards and retry functionality. By prototype completion, "students were lining up and asking to play again" the Roman numeral lessons.

---

## Three Core Findings

### 1. Acknowledgement Trumps Prizes
- Original approach: Avatar and collection systems
- Revised approach: Simple checkboxes/stars earned after four consecutive correct answers

Students prioritized rewards directly linked to academic performance over distant collectible items. Checkboxes represented competency — what students genuinely valued.

### 2. Localize Reward Timing
- Original approach: Rewards distributed at lesson completion
- Revised approach: Immediate rewards following each question

Students lack long-term probabilistic thinking. Instant feedback made individual questions feel consequential and enabled goal-setting around achievable milestones.

### 3. Enable Repeated Attempts
- Original approach: Progress forward regardless of performance
- Revised approach: Unlimited retries until correct answers achieved

When students couldn't retry, engagement plummeted after initial failures. One student even cleared his browser cache rather than accept an incorrect mark — demonstrating how much correction mattered to learners.
