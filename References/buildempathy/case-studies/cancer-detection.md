# Cancer Detection Improvement

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/cancerdetection  
**Client:** Diagnostics for All (startup, paper-based diagnostic tools for developing regions)  
**Challenge:** Color-matching accuracy — users struggled with original design

---

## Key Outcome

"We reduced error rates from over 5% to 0%. We increased patients' confidence to 10/10."

---

## Methodology

Extensive prototyping — over 30 design variations tested within one week.

---

## Design Evolution

**Initial Problem:**  
The original diagnostic guide prioritized numbers over colors, included separated instructions users ignored, and presented colors at a small scale against extensive gray space.

**First Iteration (failed):**  
Adding more color options and embedding instructions backfired — users became confused by gradient options lacking exact matches, and medical terminology created additional barriers.

**Final Solution:**
- Instructions embedded sequentially starting with Step 1
- International emoji indicators (smiling/frowning faces) replacing medical jargon
- A physical slit in the gradient encouraging color-to-color overlay
- Unobstructed gradient allowing precise color matching

---

## Key Insight

Direct manipulation — overlaying test strips — significantly improved accuracy when the tool's design naturally encouraged this behavior.
