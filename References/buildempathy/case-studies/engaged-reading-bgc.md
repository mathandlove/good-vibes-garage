# Creating an Engaging Reading Experience — Boys & Girls Club

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/engagedreading  
**Scraped:** April 2026

---

## Overview

mPath worked with the Boys & Girls Club to design reading lessons for reluctant 3rd and 4th graders that would motivate active engagement.

**Primary Outcome:** "Through a series of prototypes, we designed reading lessons where students would actively read each line, pay attention to feedback, and ask to do it again."

**Team:** Garrett Hedman, Meredith Wilson, Christine Zanchi, Elysa Greenberger, Sara Stump

---

## Three Key Design Interventions

### 1. Add "Weight" to Questions
- **Problem:** Students showed little concern about incorrect answers and rarely read texts thoroughly.
- **Solution:** Introduced a coin reward system — students earned one coin per correct answer within two guesses, creating tangible motivation.

### 2. Remove Visual Clutter
- **Problem:** Eye tracking revealed students skipped text to focus on questions and images instead.
- **Solution:** Simplified the interface to show only one to two sentences at a time, with questions and images appearing only after reading completion.

### 3. Bring in Big Questions
- **Problem:** Struggling readers lacked purpose beyond finishing quickly.
- **Solution:** Added opening "Wonder Questions" (example: "Why do vultures eat dead animals?") followed by peer discussion to build curiosity and engagement.

---

## Additional Insights
- Peer discussion proved more effective than typing responses
- Skip-alerts helped students recognize their own reading avoidance without causing frustration
- Simple cartoons reduced overwhelm while supporting comprehension
- Wonder Questions work best when adults find them genuinely interesting
- Pre-reading games proved more effective than post-reading rewards for engagement
