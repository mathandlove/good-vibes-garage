# Teaching Phonemic Awareness Digitally

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/addition-lbmkg  
**Note:** URL slug is `addition-lbmkg` but content is about phonemic awareness instruction

---

## Overview

mPath redesigned digital phonemic awareness instruction to mirror effective small-group teaching practices with direct feedback and modeling.

---

## Primary Outcome

The team delivered ready-to-implement lessons enabling children to practice sounds with clear feedback mechanisms.

---

## Key Findings and Changes

### Challenge Reframing
**Original approach:** Quiz-like activities ("Put the word that starts with B back in the box") created student anxiety and confusion.

**Solution:** Reframed challenges around agency and helping (e.g., "Help feed the dog"), which significantly reduced anxiety and increased engagement.

### Speaking Integration
**Original approach:** Students spoke aloud only once per lesson, despite phonemic awareness research emphasizing oral practice.

**Solution:** Incorporated speaking at every step — every phoneme, word, and blend — recognizing kindergarteners don't distinguish between speaking and clicking.

### Simulation Scaffolding
**Original approach:** "Feed the Monster" activity immediately presented unfamiliar interface concepts, confusing students.

**Solution:** Started simulations with familiar concepts (like differentiating animal sounds) before introducing new skills.
