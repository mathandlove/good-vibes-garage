# Adding Rewards to Digital Lessons — Boys & Girls Club

**Source URL:** https://www.buildempathy.com/casestudies/learningrewards  
**Scraped:** April 2026

**Team:** Garrett Hedman, Meredith Wilson, Christine Zanchi, Elysa Greenberger, Elliott Hedman

---

## Overview

mPath partnered with the Boys & Girls Club to research how rewards and framing could enhance student motivation during digital lessons.

**Key Outcome:** Instead of implementing a complex avatar system, research guided the client toward immediate local rewards after questions and a "try-it-again" feature. The prototype proved so engaging that students requested additional roman numeral lessons.

---

## Three Core Findings

### 1. Acknowledgment Over Prizes
- Initial approach: Avatar and collection systems
- Finding: "Students cared more about rewards more closely tied to their academic achievements"
- Solution: Simple checkboxes/stars representing competency rather than cosmetic rewards

### 2. Immediate Rewards
- Initial approach: Rewards given at lesson completion
- Finding: End-of-lesson incentives didn't improve effort or retention
- Solution: Rewards delivered immediately after each question

### 3. Try-It-Again is Essential
- Initial approach: Standard online lessons advancing despite incorrect answers
- Finding: Students disengaged when unable to demonstrate mastery
- Solution: Unlimited retry attempts enabling goal completion and achievement

---

## Critical Insights
- Elementary students struggle forming goals around avatar collection
- Children prioritize direct academic acknowledgment over indirect gamification
- The framework aligns with Deci's model for intrinsic motivation
- Choice in retrying tasks significantly increases reengagement
