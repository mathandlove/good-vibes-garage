# Empowering Parents to Teach Reading — Toddlers Can Read

**Scraped:** April 2026

**Client:** Toddlers Can Read  
**Sector:** App Design, Education, Parents, Motivation  
**Role:** Design and Development Lead

---

## Context

Spencer, founder of Toddlers Can Read, sought to increase adoption and success rates of his educational video library by developing an interactive app to inspire parental action in literacy instruction.

---

## Design Review Findings

Three key improvement opportunities identified:
- **People Learn by Doing:** Two-hour video prerequisites created barriers to entry
- **Make Parent Actions Tangible:** Activities lacked clarity on implementation
- **Badges Only Work with Meaningful Goals:** Incentives misaligned with parent objectives around teaching reading

---

## Methodology

1. **Brainstorming & Prototyping:** Team conducted collaborative sessions resulting in Prototype #1
2. **Digital Development:** Built web-based prototype using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for shareability and adaptive functionality
3. **At-Home Testing:** Prototypes distributed to parents without instructions; open-ended interviews conducted
4. **Co-Creation:** Parents collaborated to redesign features for daily engagement

---

## Key Research Finding: Two User Profiles

- **Planners:** Highly organized, comprehensive research-oriented
- **Dreamers (majority):** Desire to help but abandon when obstacles arise

> "Ask a mother if she wants to help her child read, and she will most likely say yes. Give a teaching app to a mother, and it will likely never be used."

---

## Design Principles for Dreamer Parents

1. **Drips, Not Firehoses:** Bite-sized, sequential learning rather than overwhelming information dumps
2. **Soft Starts:** Introduce routines and game structures before educational content; begin with non-academic activities like animal sounds
3. **Eliminate Parent Choice:** Reduce decision paralysis; provide predetermined activities rather than multiple options
4. **Hackable Tasks:** Allow modifications when parental judgment necessitates changes while maintaining guided defaults
5. **Leave AI in the Background:** FAQ sections unused; parents preferred algorithmic recommendations requiring no active input
6. **Mandatory Fun:** Child engagement drives parental app usage; parental enthusiasm and failure-handling directly impact learning success

---

## Conclusion

Effective parent-empowered literacy solutions require removing expertise, planning, and entertainment burdens. Design should enable parents to feel confident in supporting early reading development without specialized academic knowledge.
