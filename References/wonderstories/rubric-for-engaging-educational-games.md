# A Rubric for Making Engaging Educational Games
**Source:** https://bagpipe-icosahedron-ln8h.squarespace.com/research/engaginggamesrubric
**Author:** Elliott Hedman
**Date:** May 15, 2023
**Scraped:** 2026-04-17

---

At my research company, we have developed a game approach based on years of experience and research.

Over the last 10 years, I've been hired to review and create educational games for many top educational and toy companies. Determining what makes educational games effective has been challenging.

A persistent problem with educational game design: most fail because designers assume children will accept games they themselves wouldn't play. When children have access to million-dollar commercial apps at home, educational games must compete on entertainment value.

Here are the **5 Foundational Pieces that will determine the effectiveness and fun of an educational game.**

---

## 1. Game Mechanics

Game mechanics drive engagement and teaching effectiveness. Games should include a clear winning goal and progressive difficulty levels. Additionally, games should incorporate at least one of these elements:

- A non-academic interactive skill requiring effort to master without causing frustration
- A clear, understandable timing element that children can overcome through effort
- Opportunities for children to create something reflecting their own contribution (noted as most challenging to design)

---

## 2. Sticky Stories

Story matters, particularly for younger learners. Stories should acknowledge mistakes and connect them to learning objectives. Optimal stories are "memorable and silly, yet easy to understand, and not so long that it becomes confusing." The ideal length is "two to three minutes in length total."

---

## 3. Agency: The Child's Actions Affect the Story

Children's choices should meaningfully impact the narrative rather than simply affecting scoreboards. "Characters in the story should react to the child's choices." A basic example: characters respond emotionally to correct or incorrect answers.

---

## 4. Educational Effectiveness

Games should help children master new skills or concepts through repetition and multiple answer attempts with constructive feedback. "The majority of the time spent playing the game should be focused on practicing and mastering skills."

Teachers should introduce new concepts, while games provide practice support. Children enjoy learning but become demotivated if they cannot adequately practice.

---

## 5. Usability/Flow

The interface requires intuitive, easy-to-use controls with professional-quality graphics, animations, and text. Transitions should feel polished without excessive loading times or navigation problems.

---

## Conclusion

Incorporating these elements significantly increases game effectiveness for children's learning. A corresponding rubric was developed to help developers evaluate engagement levels, ensuring games are "engaging, memorable, and educationally effective."
