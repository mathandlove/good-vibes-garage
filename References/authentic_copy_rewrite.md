## About Section ✅

Hey, I'm Elliott. I've spent my career in two worlds — building new things (Google, IDEO, LEGO, MIT Media Lab) and understanding people. I used to run a consultancy helping companies prototype ideas fast. That was fun, but I kept noticing something bigger.

Most people have no idea what vibe coding is. They don't know that right now, today, they could describe an idea and watch it become real in 30 minutes. Not engineers — I'm talking about teachers, nonprofit directors, community organizers. People with visions and no way to build them.

That gap isn't an engineering problem. It's a mindset problem. And I'm fascinated by it.

So that's what this is. I'm sitting down with people, one at a time, and showing them what's possible. I'm still early. But every time someone goes from "there's no way I can do this" to building the whole thing on their own — I know I'm in the right place.

## Things I've Built ✅

**Section intro:**
Here's what I've been building along the way. Each one started as an idea I couldn't stop thinking about.

**Wonder Stories**
Working at the Boys & Girls Club, I noticed the kids loved Kahoot! and absolutely despised reading. I wanted to create a reading experience that could compete in their Marvel and Fortnite world. 26,000 active users now — and parents of kids with dyslexia keep telling me their children won't read anything else.

**Robodexo**
I had a week off work, a 3-month-old, and my 40th birthday coming up. I always throw elaborate murder mysteries. This time I wanted everyone to be a Pokemon trainer — playing cards with QR codes, quests, tournaments, discovery. Built the whole thing in a week. 70 people came.

**Level UP**
My first attempt to help students use AI to improve their actual writing ability. This was before Claude Code, and it was hard. But it taught me a lot about what this kind of teaching needs.

**Toddlers Can Read**
Partnered with Spencer Russell to build a mock app and test it in a week. We wanted to find out what busy parents actually need to teach their kids to read. The answer: clear structure and extreme fun.

**Play Extremes**
I had a game idea. Built it in a day using only vibe coding. No reason other than: why not?
